Our Journey

History & Development

From a founding act to a thriving 300-acre campus — the story of CUSB's growth.

2009–2013
Foundation — Central University of Bihar

Established under the Central Universities Act, 2009 (Section 25), the university began from a single room at Hindi Bhawan, Patna, with the first Vice-Chancellor Prof. Janak Pandey joining on 2nd March, 2009.

2009–10: Centre for Development Studies founded; first PG programme launched under the School of Social Sciences and Policy.

2010–11: Five PG programmes added — Biotechnology, Computer Science, Environmental Sciences, Mathematics, and Statistics.

2011–12: Bioinformatics, Communication & Media Studies, and Psychology added. Seven more followed: Life Science, Economics, English, Hindi, Political Science & International Relations, Sociology, and MTech (CS).

2013: First Convocation held 26th September — graced by Vice President M. Hamid Ansari; 156 graduates from the first three batches were awarded degrees.

I
II
2013–2014
Renamed to CUSB

The Central Universities (Amendment) Act, 2014 renamed the institution to Central University of South Bihar — better reflecting its regional identity and scope.

Integrated BA.BEd; BSc.BEd; BA.LLB (Hons); BSc.LLB (Hons); and Integrated M.Phil–Ph.D. programmes in Bioinformatics, Biotechnology, CS, Development Studies, Economics, Environmental Science, Hindi, Life Science, Mathematics, Political Science & IR, and Statistics were introduced. (Integrated MPhil–PhD was replaced with PhD w.e.f. AY 2016–17.)

2014–2018
Permanent Campus & Expansion

Shifted to its permanent 300-acre campus at Panchanpur, Gaya. New schools, departments, and infrastructure including the Central Instrumental Facility were commissioned.

2014–15: B.Voc (Arts & Crafts) introduced under School of Vocational Studies (withdrawn from 2017–18 for technical reasons).

2016–17: LLM launched under School of Law & Governance. Ph.D. programmes started across 17 departments including Biotechnology, Life Science, Environmental Science, Mathematics, Statistics, CS, Hindi, English, Sociology, Economics, Political Science & IR, Education, Law, and Bioinformatics.

2017–18: M.Ed. programme introduced under Department of Teacher Education.

2018–19: Five new PG programmes: Chemistry, Physics, Social Work, M.Com, and History.

2019–20: Ph.D. in Chemistry added under School of Physical & Chemical Sciences.

2023
NAAC Accreditation

CUSB received NAAC accreditation, recognising its commitment to quality education, research excellence, and institutional governance — a milestone affirming the university's academic standing on the national stage.

2026
Towards Future Excellence

Admissions open for 2026–27 under NEP 2020. CUSB grows with new ITEP programmes and robust research pathways via CUET-PG routes — shaping the next generation of scholars from South Bihar.
